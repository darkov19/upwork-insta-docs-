What We Are Building

A subscription-based web application at app.domain.com that lets your customers monitor public Instagram accounts — tracking followers and unfollowers after each sync. Four modules we build, plugging into your existing payment and auth setup:

1
Target Management
Add public Instagram accounts to monitor. Validates each account (public? within follower limit?). Shows status — active, paused-private, paused-out-of-plan, error.
2
Sync Engine
Fetches follower and following lists from Instagram, computes exactly who followed and unfollowed since the last sync, and stores only the changes. Runs as a background job — no browser timeouts.
3
Dashboard + Activity Feed
Main app screen showing monitored targets, sync status (live update — no page refresh needed), and a 90-day activity feed of who followed and unfollowed.
4
Story Viewer + Weekly Email
Plan-gated story viewer with intelligent caching (stories cached for exactly their remaining Instagram lifetime). Weekly email summary sent only when there is activity to report.
Domain setup: Your existing landing page at domain.com stays completely untouched. We build the app at app.domain.com. The only handoff required: after a user logs in, they are redirected to app.domain.com/dashboard — our app takes it from there.

Risks & Mitigations

⚡
API rate limits under concurrent load
Many users syncing simultaneously share one API key. Handled: Rate limiting is enforced automatically across all concurrent jobs, with automatic retry on failure. No user-facing errors.
🔒
Duplicate sync on the same account
Two simultaneous sync requests for the same target could produce duplicate events. Handled: Database-level locking ensures only one sync runs per target at a time. The second request is cleanly rejected.
⏱
Sync timeout on large accounts
Syncing a large account can take 60–90 seconds — too long for a standard web request. Handled: Syncs run as background jobs with no timeout. The browser updates automatically the moment the job completes.
📊
Account goes private after being added
A monitored account could switch to private after being added. Handled: Detected automatically on the next sync. The target is paused with a clear status message. Reactivated automatically if the account goes public again.
💳
Payment webhook missed or delivered twice
Stripe retries failed webhooks, which can result in duplicate delivery. Handled: Duplicate events are detected and discarded automatically — no double-charges or double-registrations.
