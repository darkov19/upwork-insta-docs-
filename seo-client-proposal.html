What We Are Building

A full-stack local SEO SaaS platform for agencies — the same product category as localrank.so, with meaningful technical improvements in the areas that matter most to your agency clients. The platform has six modules that work together:

1
Local Rank Tracker
Shows exactly where a business ranks on Google Maps across a geographic area — not a single number, but a visual grid showing rank at every point in the target zone.
2
AI Recommendation Monitor
Automatically asks ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity whether they recommend a business — and gives a weighted score agencies can show clients month after month.
3
LLM Citation Builder
Generates "Best X in City" articles featuring the client's business and distributes them to news sites so AI models can find and cite them.
4
Local Citations Builder
Submits the business's name, address, and phone to the 20 highest-authority directories in one click — the traditional foundation of local SEO. Additional directories added post-launch.
5
GBP Automation
Manages Google Business Profiles for multiple clients from one dashboard — posts, review replies, and review collection campaigns.
6
Local Leads Database
A prospecting tool that lets the agency find new business clients by searching 210 million+ contacts by location, industry, and job title.

Visibility score (% of models recommending) ✓ Improved We go further — see note below.

Important improvement over your spec: Your spec defines visibility as a simple percentage (e.g. 2/4 models = 50%). We use a smarter four-component scoring system:
  • Rank position — 40% weight
  • Mention rate — 30% weight
  • Share of voice — 20% weight
  • Sentiment — 10% weight

A rank #1 glowing recommendation scores ~78%. A negative buried mention scores ~19%. Both would score 50% under a simple percentage. This is a significant differentiator over localrank.so.

Also: We use live web search on all four AI providers. The models search the current web when answering — not stored training data from 2023. A business that improved its local SEO last month will be scored correctly.

Module 3 — LLM Citation Builder

Your Requirement Status How
Campaign setup (keyword + brand + destination URL) ✓ Full Simple campaign form.
Real-time status tracking ("501/501 Published") ✓ Full Live counter updates as articles are distributed.
Network distribution (PBN or mass PR distribution) ✓ Full Via PR wire distribution — see important note below.
Important note on distribution — please read:

Your requirements mention two options: a Private Blog Network (PBN) or mass PR distribution tools. We have chosen PR wire distribution for the following reasons:
  • A PBN means owning and maintaining hundreds of websites — a separate months-long project on its own.
  • PR wire services (e.g. EIN Presswire) already have 200–500 real, indexed news and media sites. One API call publishes your article across all of them within hours.
  • Real news sites carry stronger domain authority than self-built blogs — better indexation and more credibility with AI tools like Perplexity.
  • PR wire distribution is a legitimate, Google-approved practice. PBNs carry Google penalty risk.

What this means for you: You create an account with EIN Presswire (free tier available), enter your API key in the platform once, and the platform handles everything. The cost per distribution job (~$50–$99) is paid directly from your account to the PR wire service — not charged through the platform.

This approach is actually better than what localrank.so has built. Their 550 self-built domains carry real Google penalty risk. Your platform uses real, credible news sites.

How This Compares to localrank.so

You shared localrank.so as the reference product. We have done a full audit of their platform including a live authenticated account. Here is the honest comparison.

Where Our Platform is Stronger

  • AI Monitor scoring (Module 2)
    We use a four-component weighted score (rank 40%, mention 30%, share of voice 20%, sentiment 10%). Their score is a binary percentage — 2 out of 4 models = 50%, regardless of whether the mention was glowing or negative.
  • Live web search on all 4 AI providers
    Their platform queries AI on training data from October 2023. Ours uses live web search — scores reflect the current web, not two-year-old data.
  • Leads Database: 14× more data
    Their database has 15 million contacts. Ours uses Apollo.io with 210 million+.
  • Real-time scan progress
    Users watch the map fill in pin by pin as each grid point is scanned. Their platform shows a spinner until it's done.
  • Infrastructure reliability
    Their entire platform runs on a single self-managed server. Ours runs on managed cloud infrastructure with automatic failover and scaling.

Where They Are Currently Ahead

  • Platform is live today
    They have paying customers. Ours is being built.
  • 550 citation directories
    Our Phase 1 targets the 20 highest-authority directories (the ones that actually move rankings). Expanding to 100+ is Phase 2.
On the citation directory gap: More directories does not always mean more value. The top 20 high-authority directories (Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages, Foursquare, etc.) account for the vast majority of citation ranking weight. Their 550 directories include a long tail of low-authority sites with near-zero SEO impact. Additionally, their self-built blog network carries real Google penalty risk that our PR wire approach does not.

A Word on Risk

Building software always carries some uncertainty. We want to be transparent about the main risks and how we have planned for them.

⚙
Directory automation (Module 4)
Submitting to directories automatically requires a browser that visits each site and fills out forms. Phase 1 covers 20 high-authority directories. Some directories occasionally update their website design, which can break a script temporarily. Any directory that cannot be automated is flagged in the dashboard with a link for manual submission — no single directory blocks the platform from working. Expanding beyond 20 is a post-launch effort.
📡
PR wire account (Module 3)
Module 3 requires you to have an EIN Presswire account with API access. This is a simple self-service sign-up — not a technical blocker. We will remind you before we reach that milestone.
🔗
Apollo.io API (Module 6)
The Leads Database depends on Apollo.io's API. We need to confirm the API access tier that suits your expected usage volume before we build this module.
